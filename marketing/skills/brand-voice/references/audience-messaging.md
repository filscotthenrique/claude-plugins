# Audience-Specific Messaging

Elevator pitches, pain point matrices, and messaging hooks organized by persona. Use these when writing content targeted at a specific audience segment.

## Positioning statement (internal reference)

> **For student pilots and flight instructors** who are frustrated by fragmented, outdated tools that weren't designed for them, **Abeam is a pilot-first platform** that brings logbook, currency tracking, weather, scheduling, and progress into one modern app. **Unlike Flight Schedule Pro and LogTen Pro**, which serve school administrators or power users on a single platform, **Abeam starts with the people actually flying** — and grows with them from first lesson to flight school management.

## Elevator pitches

### For student pilots (30 seconds)

> Abeam is a free app that puts your whole flying life in one place — your logbook, your currency, live weather at your home airport, your aircraft, and your certificates. It works on your iPhone, your iPad, and the web, so your data is always with you. We built it because we're pilots too, and we were tired of juggling five different apps just to go flying.

### For flight instructors (30 seconds)

> Abeam starts as a pilot logbook and dashboard, but we're building a full instructor platform — student management, digital endorsements, scheduling, debriefs, billing. Right now your students can use Abeam Pilot for free, and when you upgrade to Abeam Instruct, you get instructor tools that were actually built for how independent CFIs work. No more Google Calendar and Venmo.

### For flight school operators (30 seconds — Phase 3)

> Abeam is a modern platform for flight schools that your instructors and students actually want to use — because many of them already do. We start with free pilot tools, add instructor management, and then give schools scheduling, billing, and fleet management at transparent pricing with no contracts. Your people are already on Abeam. Now you can connect them.

### For investors/press (30 seconds)

> Abeam is a platform for general aviation growing from the bottom up. We give pilots free tools — logbook, currency, weather — then expand into paid instructor and flight school management. Our go-to-market flips the industry: instead of selling to school admins and forcing adoption, we build loyalty with individuals first. The flight training software market is fragmented, outdated, and ripe for a modern, mobile-first platform.

## Messaging hooks by persona

### Student pilots — what resonates

- "Free to fly with. No hour limits. No bait-and-switch."
- "Your logbook, your currency, your weather — one app, on your iPhone and the web"
- "Built for the way you fly"
- "Log a flight in under 30 seconds"

**Channels:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube (discovery); Reddit r/flying, r/StudentPilots (research); App Store search (high-intent); CFI recommendation (most trusted)

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, not corporate. Respectful of their investment. Never condescending. Enthusiastic without being cheesy.

### Active private pilots — what resonates

- "Never wonder 'am I current?' again"
- "Your data follows you. Not the other way around."
- ~~"Finally, a pilot app that doesn't care what phone you carry"~~ (do not use — implies Android support)
- "Your logbook shouldn't cost more than avgas"

**Channels:** Pilots of America, Reddit r/flying (trusted communities); YouTube (comparison content); in-person at fly-ins, EAA meetings

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer (they're experienced). Factual and fair about competitors. Understated. Respect their time and intelligence.

### Flight instructors — what resonates

- "There's a better way to run your CFI business"
- "The first app built for the way you actually work"
- "Get your non-flying hours back"
- "See every student's journey at a glance"

**Channels:** LinkedIn (professional presence); r/CFI; NAFI events; direct outreach; podcast appearances

**Tone:** Empathize with the admin burden. Acknowledge thin margins. Speak to the reality of independent instruction. Professional but warm.
