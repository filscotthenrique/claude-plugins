# Abeam Competitive Context

Use this for internal understanding and when explicitly writing comparison content. The default in marketing copy is to focus on Abeam — not to name competitors.

## Competitor mention rules (critical)

**Default: do not mention competitors by name in marketing copy.** Focus on what Abeam does. Let pilots draw their own comparisons.

Only name a competitor when ALL of these are true:
1. The user explicitly asks for a comparison or the content format requires it.
2. You can verify the claim from the facts below. Do not guess. If it's not listed here, say "I'm not sure — worth checking their site."
3. The comparison highlights something Abeam does better or differently.

## ForeFlight

**What it is:** The dominant EFB (Electronic Flight Bag) for iOS. Flight planning, charts, weather, and a bolted-on logbook. ~135K Instagram followers. The 800-pound gorilla.

**Key facts:**
- **Platform:** iOS only. No Android. No web logbook.
- **Pricing:** $120-$240/year depending on tier.
- **Ownership:** Sold to Thoma Bravo (private equity) in November 2025 for $10.55B. Layoffs followed in January 2026.
- **Logbook:** Exists but is an afterthought — not a dedicated logbook product. Pilots describe it as "bolted on."
- **Instructor features:** CFI referral program, endorsement templates (60+ from AC 61-65H), remote signing. Instructor features are decent but buried inside a $200/yr flight planning app.
- **Strengths:** Brand recognition, polish, EFB features, chart coverage, massive user base.
- **Vulnerabilities:** iOS-only, subscription cost, PE ownership generating community anxiety, email-only support, no TikTok presence, logbook is secondary to EFB.

**Abeam's angle:** Abeam is a logbook-first platform. ForeFlight is a flight planning tool with a logbook added on. Different products for different priorities.

## LogTen Pro / Pilotbase

**What it is:** Dedicated digital logbook. 160,000+ pilots. Now merging with Flight Schedule Pro under the "Pilotbase" parent brand (launched February 2026).

**Key facts:**
- **Platform:** iOS only. No Android. No web.
- **Pricing:** $80-$130/year. Subscription model generates significant backlash.
- **Ownership:** Acquired by Flight Schedule Pro parent in May 2022. Rebranding to Pilotbase.
- **Strengths:** Career logbook features (airline-focused), airline cadet program partnerships (Delta, SkyWest, Republic — 1,771+ subscribers in 2023), diversity org partnerships (NGPA, Latino Pilots Association).
- **Vulnerabilities:** iOS-only, subscription pricing backlash, rebrand confusion, community uncertainty about direction.

**Abeam's angle:** Free logbook with modern UX on iOS and web. No subscription required for core features. Data portability.

## Flight Schedule Pro (FSP)

**What it is:** Flight school management platform. 1,400+ schools. B2B model.

**Key facts:**
- **Platform:** Web + limited iOS app. School-facing, not pilot-facing.
- **Pricing:** Opaque — requires sales conversation. Schools report $100-300+/month.
- **Strengths:** Market leader for flight school scheduling. Sallie Mae financing partnership. Strong event presence (8+ conferences/year).
- **Vulnerabilities:** Pricing opacity, school-admin focus (not instructor or pilot friendly), desktop-oriented UX.

**Abeam's angle (Phase 3):** Bottom-up adoption. Pilots and instructors already using Abeam individually → schools connect them. Transparent pricing vs FSP's "talk to sales."

## MyFlightbook

**What it is:** Free, open-source, donation-supported logbook. Built by solo developer Eric Berman. 24.87M flights logged across 369,000 aircraft in 161 countries.

**Key facts:**
- **Platform:** iOS, Android, and Web. The only cross-platform option with Android.
- **Pricing:** Completely free. Donation-supported.
- **Strengths:** Free, cross-platform, massive dataset, developer personally active on forums (gold standard for community engagement), 4.93 App Store rating.
- **Vulnerabilities:** "Pretty vintage 1990s UI" (developer's own words). Minimal instructor features. No marketing.

**Abeam's angle:** Modern design + free core features. Abeam occupies the empty quadrant: modern AND affordable. MyFlightbook is free but dated; ForeFlight is polished but expensive.

## Flight Circle

**What it is:** Scheduling tool for flying clubs and small schools. Simple, affordable.

**Key facts:**
- **Platform:** Web-based.
- **Pricing:** $10/month per aircraft. Transparent.
- **Strengths:** Simplicity, affordability, EAA benefits partnership, warm community reviews.
- **Vulnerabilities:** Limited features, no mobile app, minimal marketing.

## Pilot Partner

**What it is:** Pilot logbook + flying club management. 3-4 person team in Austin, TX. Operates its own flying club as a testbed.

**Key facts:**
- **Platform:** iOS, Android, and Web.
- **Pricing:** Free for pilots; CFI features via referral program.
- **Strengths:** Innovative student self-debrief workflow, ACS-based progress tracking, founder active in forums.
- **Vulnerabilities:** Small user base, dated feel, referral pricing creates friction.

## Market-wide themes to leverage

**Subscription fatigue:** Pilots are tired of paying $100-300/year across multiple apps. Abeam's free tier directly counters this.

**iOS lock-in:** ForeFlight and LogTen are both iOS-only. Abeam on iOS + web is a step toward platform freedom. (Android is roadmap, not reality — don't overclaim.)

**PE anxiety:** Thoma Bravo buying ForeFlight and FSP/LogTen merging into Pilotbase creates uncertainty. "Built by a pilot, not a private equity firm" resonates — but use it factually, not as a jab.

**Community > budget:** MyFlightbook proves that personal engagement beats marketing spend. Abeam's founder-led community approach follows this playbook.

**CFI workflow gap:** No tool combines student CRM + progress tracking + debrief + endorsements + scheduling + invoicing for independent CFIs. This is Abeam Instruct's open lane.
