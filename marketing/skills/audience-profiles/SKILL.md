# Abeam Audience Profiles

Five personas drive Abeam's marketing. The first two are the primary audience for the Q2 2026 launch of Abeam Pilot. The remaining three serve Q3–Q4 when Abeam Instruct and flight school tools launch.

## Persona 1: Student Pilot (Launch Audience)

**Who they are:** Age 18–35, college student or early career professional, pre-solo through checkride prep. Spending $10,000–20,000+ on training. Uses iPhone and/or iPad. Discovers tools through TikTok, Instagram, Reddit, YouTube, and CFI recommendations.

**What they care about:** Cost (free is essential), simplicity (must be intuitive on first open), cross-platform (phone + web), and community (wants to belong to something). Every dollar and every endorsement matters to them.

**Top pain points:**
- Juggling 5+ apps for logbook, weather, documents, scheduling, and study
- No good free logbook with modern UX
- Confusion about currency and medical requirements
- Cost opacity — never sure how much training has actually cost
- Instructor turnover means lost context

**Messaging that works:**
- "Free to fly with. No hour limits. No bait-and-switch."
- "Your logbook, your currency, your weather — one app, every device"
- "Log a flight in under 30 seconds"
- Emphasize FREE. Emphasize SIMPLE. Never talk down about experience level.

**Tone:** Warm, encouraging, peer-to-peer. Respectful of the financial and emotional weight of training.

**Channels:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube (discovery). Reddit r/flying, r/StudentPilots (validation). App Store search (high-intent). CFI recommendation (most trusted).

## Persona 2: Active Private Pilot (Retention Audience)

**Who they are:** Age 30–55, working professional, PPL or instrument rated. Flies 2–8 times per month. Rents from a club or co-owns. iPhone primary, iPad in cockpit.

**What they care about:** Value (does this replace something I already pay for?), data portability (can I import/export?), cross-platform, and quality (they've used ForeFlight — they expect polish).

**Top pain points:**
- Currency anxiety ("I had to cancel a flight because I wasn't sure if my night currency was valid")
- Subscription fatigue ($200/yr ForeFlight + $130/yr LogTen)
- Platform lock-in (all data in ForeFlight/LogTen, can't leave)
- ForeFlight's logbook is an afterthought bolted onto flight planning

**Messaging that works:**
- "Never wonder 'am I current?' again"
- "Your data follows you. Not the other way around."
- "Your logbook shouldn't cost more than avgas"
- Understated, factual. They know what they want — don't oversell.

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, factual and fair about competitors, understated. Respect their time and intelligence.

**Channels:** Pilots of America, Reddit r/flying (trusted communities). YouTube comparisons. LinkedIn. In-person at fly-ins and EAA meetings.

## Persona 3: Flight School Owner (Phase 3 — Q4 2026)

**Who they are:** Age 45–65, former CFI or commercial pilot. 1–10 aircraft, 5–15 instructors, 30–80 active students. $300K–$2M annual revenue.

**What they care about:** ROI, aircraft utilization, instructor retention, student completion rates, and reducing admin overhead. Thin margins — every dollar counts.

**Top pain points:**
- Scheduling is a mess and planes sit idle
- More time on admin than flying or teaching
- Current software too expensive, too complicated, or desktop-only
- Opaque pricing ("Why do I have to talk to sales?")

**Messaging that works:**
- "Modern software designed for Part 61 schools"
- "Your people are already on Abeam. Now you can connect them." (bottom-up adoption narrative)
- ROI-oriented: time saved, utilization improved, transparent pricing

**Tone:** Professional but not corporate. Respect for aviation expertise. Peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-customer.

**Channels:** Email, phone (scheduled calls), aviation forums (lurks), in-person at aviation events. AOPA, Flying Magazine, aviation podcasts.

## Persona 4: Flight School Manager / Chief Instructor (Phase 3)

**Who they are:** Age 30–55, experienced CFI with 1,000–5,000 hours. Manages day-to-day operations. Key influencer — if they don't like it, the purchase doesn't happen.

**What they care about:** Efficiency, student success, mobile access, and ease of use. They're juggling teaching, admin, and flying.

**Top pain points:**
- Half the day spent managing schedules (phone calls, texts, whiteboard updates)
- Instructors don't update availability
- No centralized student progression tracking
- Desktop-only systems that can't be used from the flight line

**Messaging that works:**
- "Get back to teaching instead of coordinating schedules"
- "Update schedules from anywhere — you're not chained to the desk"
- "Students can see availability and book themselves — no more phone tag"

**Tone:** Practical, solutions-oriented. Concise. Empathetic to daily challenges.

## Persona 5: Independent CFI / Small Flying Club Manager (Phase 3)

**Who they are:** CFI building hours (age 25–45) or volunteer club manager (age 40–65). 1–2 aircraft, 5–15 students or 30–150 club members. Extremely price-sensitive.

**What they care about:** Affordability above all. Simplicity. DIY mindset — willing to figure things out themselves.

**Top pain points:**
- Manual billing eats hours every month
- Enterprise software costs more than they make
- Scheduling via group texts and shared Google calendars
- "All-in-one" solutions have features they don't need (paying for bloat)

**Messaging that works:**
- "Stop spending hours on manual billing and scheduling"
- "Set up your account in minutes, not days"
- "Month-to-month, cancel anytime"
- Lead with price. Show the $29/month number early.

**Tone:** No-nonsense, respectful of budget constraints, supportive.

## When writing for a specific audience

1. Check the persona's pain points — lead with one they'd recognize.
2. Match their tone expectations (student = warm; private pilot = understated; school owner = professional).
3. Use their preferred channels' formatting conventions.
4. Reference their buying behavior to calibrate the CTA (students: "free, no credit card"; school owners: "schedule a demo or try free for 30 days").
