# Abeam Product Knowledge

**Last updated:** March 2026 (pre-launch)
**Launch target:** Late June 2026

Keep all marketing copy accurate to what's described below. Do not overclaim features that aren't live. If unsure whether a feature exists, say so rather than guess.

## Product line

Abeam has three products at different stages:

**Abeam Pilot** (Free tier — launching Q2 2026)
The core pilot app. Free for all pilots. This is the launch product.

**Abeam Instruct** (Paid — launching Q3–Q4 2026)
Instructor tools that add onto Abeam Pilot. Student management, endorsements, resource sharing.

**Abeam Instruct App** (Planned)
A dedicated app for independent CFIs — their CRM, student dashboard, and business tool.

## Platform availability

- **iOS** (iPhone and iPad): Yes
- **Web**: Yes
- **Android**: No. Long-term roadmap, likely tied to European market expansion. Do not mention Android availability in marketing copy. Be honest if asked: "It's on the roadmap but not available yet."

"Works on your iPhone, your iPad, and the web" — accurate.
"Works on every device" — not accurate.

## Abeam Pilot features (launch)

**Digital logbook**
- Log flights in seconds with full Part 61 support
- All standard logbook fields (PIC, SIC, dual received/given, instrument, night, cross-country, etc.)
- Import from other logbooks (CSV)
- Export your data anytime (your data stays with you)

**Currency tracking**
- Upload your medical certificate once — Abeam tracks expiry automatically
- BFR / Flight Review due date tracking
- Night currency tracking
- IFR currency tracking (if applicable)
- Dashboard shows current/not-current status at a glance
- No manual calculation needed

**Live weather (METAR)**
- Set your home airport
- Current METAR conditions on your dashboard every time you open the app
- No extra apps, no extra taps

**Digital hangar**
- Add every aircraft you fly — whether you rent, own, or share
- Store tail number, aircraft details, and maintenance notes
- Link aircraft directly to logbook entries
- Manage multiple aircraft across clubs and schools

**Certificate storage**
- Upload and store pilot certificates, medical certificates, and endorsements
- Digital copies always accessible

**Built-in references**
- FARs, AIM, and essential regulatory documents
- Searchable, always in your pocket
- No more Googling the regs on the ramp

**Dashboard**
- Flight stats and totals at a glance
- Currency status (medical, BFR, night, IFR)
- Home airport weather
- Recent flights

## Abeam Instruct features (planned — Q3–Q4 2026)

Do not market these as available. They can be mentioned as "coming soon" or "on the roadmap."

- Student roster / CRM
- ACS-mapped student progress tracking (PPL, IR, CPL)
- Post-flight debrief workflow (structured notes linked to logbook entries)
- Digital endorsements (FAA template library from AC 61-65H)
- Instructor-student logbook sync
- Instructor resource portal (share documents and materials with students)
- Scheduling / availability management
- Invoicing and payment tracking

## Pricing

**Abeam Pilot:** Free. No hour limits. No bait-and-switch. No credit card required.

**Abeam Instruct:** Pricing TBD. Positioned to be affordable for CFIs building hours on thin margins. "Less than a single ground lesson."

**Abeam for schools:** Pricing TBD. Transparent — no "talk to sales" required. Monthly, no contracts.

## Origin story

Abeam is built by Fil, a pilot in Colorado, because the existing tools weren't good enough. Solo founder. The product comes from genuine frustration with the fragmented, outdated tools available to GA pilots. This is a strength — use it naturally.

"Made at the airport, not in a boardroom."
"Built by a pilot who got tired of the same tools you're tired of."

## What NOT to claim

- No Android app exists. Don't imply it does.
- Abeam Instruct is not launched yet. Don't describe instructor features as available.
- No flight planning features (that's ForeFlight's territory, not ours).
- No ADS-B integration or synthetic vision.
- Abeam is not an EFB. It's a logbook and pilot management platform.
- Don't compare the product to ForeFlight as a direct competitor — they solve different primary problems.
