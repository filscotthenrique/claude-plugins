Draft a community response from Fil (founder of Abeam).

**Before writing anything**, load these skills by reading their SKILL.md files:
- `brand-voice` — especially the forum/Reddit tone guidance and competitor mention rules
- `product-knowledge` — for accurate product descriptions
- `competitive-context` — only if competitors are mentioned in the thread

**Gather these inputs** (ask if not provided in $ARGUMENTS):
1. **The thread or question**: Paste the post/comment being responded to
2. **Platform**: Reddit, Pilots of America, Facebook group, YouTube comment, etc.
3. **Which Abeam feature is relevant** (if any — sometimes the best response doesn't mention Abeam at all)

**Draft the response:**

- **Be helpful first.** Answer the person's actual question before mentioning Abeam. If Abeam isn't relevant to their question, don't force it.
- **Disclose affiliation.** Always: "Full disclosure, I built Abeam" or "I'm Fil, the founder of Abeam." Early in the response, not buried at the end.
- **Be honest about limitations.** If Abeam doesn't do what they need, say so. "We don't have that yet, but it's on the roadmap" is better than dodging.
- **No competitor claims you can't verify.** Don't recommend competitors for features you're not sure they have. "Worth checking what's out there" is fine. "ForeFlight has Android" is not (it doesn't).
- **Match the register.** Reddit r/flying is casual and knowledgeable. Pilots of America is slightly more formal. Facebook groups vary.
- **No hard sell.** Zero marketing smell. If mentioning Abeam feels forced in context, mention it briefly or not at all.

**Output format:**
- The response text, ready to post (no markdown formatting that wouldn't render on the platform)
- A brief note on tone choices and whether Abeam was mentioned (and why/why not)
- Reminder: "This is a suggested draft. Review and personalize before posting."
